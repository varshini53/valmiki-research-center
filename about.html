Sri Narayanam Narasimhammurthy

Founder, NCS Group (26-01-1929 to 03-09-2007)

Sri Narayanam Narasimhammurthy, the Founder Chairman of the NCS Group, was a visionary thinker whose life was deeply rooted in the spiritual and ethical teachings of the Ramayana.

He envisioned Ramanarayanam as a living embodiment of Ramayana philosophy—where culture, ethics, and spirituality converge to inspire generations. His unwavering commitment to truth, discipline, and service shaped institutions that nurture moral strength alongside intellectual growth.

Through his lifelong dedication, he laid the foundation for a spiritual and cultural legacy that continues to guide scholars, students, and seekers, reinforcing the timeless relevance of Ramayana values in modern society.

Location

A Place Where the Ramayana Lives

The Valmiki Research Centre (VRC) regards the Ramayana, composed by the great sage Valmiki, as an invaluable treasure of humanity—one of the world’s most ancient and timeless epics. Beyond religious origins, the Ramayana transcends cultural and geographical boundaries.

Ramanarayanam, the spiritual theme park, is spread across 15 acres on Korukonda Road in Vizianagaram district, Andhra Pradesh, approximately 50 km from Visakhapatnam airport. It attracts devotees and visitors from across the world.

The complex artistically presents 72 pivotal Ramayana episodes through sculptures, supported by bilingual subtitles, audio guides, and explanatory content. Temples, meditation centres, a cultural library, and themed tree groves together offer a holistic spiritual journey.

Organizational Profile

An Institution with a Cultural Mandate

The Valmiki Research Centre is a trust-based institution established under the auspices of the Ramanarayanam spiritual theme park, dedicated to integrating Ramayana teachings into formal and non-formal education.

The Centre has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the National Sanskrit University, Tirupati, and has commenced Ramayana certification programs. Diploma and Ph.D. programs in Valmiki Ramayana Studies are under development.

VRC focuses on nurturing ethical responsibility and character-building values among youth, ensuring cultural continuity across generations.
